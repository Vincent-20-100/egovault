## Core Methods

### MECE Decomposition
Every breakdown must be Mutually Exclusive (no overlap) and Collectively Exhaustive (nothing missing).

Bad: "Grow via marketing, sales, and partnerships"
Good: "Growth = (1) new acquisition, (2) existing expansion, (3) churn reduction"

### Issue Tree
Decompose the problem into sub-questions. Each leaf is answerable with data.
```
Why is revenue declining?
├── Volume? (fewer customers)
│   ├── Acquisition declining? → funnel or conversion?
│   └── Churn increasing? → product or service?
└── Price? (lower ARPU)
    ├── Mix shift to lower tier?
    └── Discounting?
```

### Hypothesis-Driven
Don't research-then-conclude. Hypothesize → identify proof/disproof data → test → confirm/pivot.
This is 10x faster than open-ended analysis.

### 80/20 (Pareto)
Before any recommendation: "If we could only do ONE thing, what would it be?"

## Frameworks (apply the RIGHT one, not all of them)

### Market & Competition

**Porter's Five Forces** — assess industry attractiveness:
New entrants / Buyer power / Supplier power / Substitutes / Rivalry → each HIGH/MED/LOW with reason.

**TAM/SAM/SOM:**
- TAM = everyone who COULD buy. SAM = everyone you CAN reach. SOM = realistic 3yr capture.
- SOM < 10x revenue target → market too small. TAM/SOM > 100x → SAM is wrong.

**Competitive Positioning:**
```
              │ Premium
    Niche     │  Differentiated
    leader    │  leader
──────────────┼──────────────
    Cost      │  STUCK IN THE
    leader    │  MIDDLE ← danger
              │ Low price
    Narrow       Broad
```

### Business Model

**Unit Economics:**
- LTV = ARPU × Gross Margin × (1/Churn). CAC = S&M Spend / New Customers.
- LTV:CAC < 1 → burning cash. 1-3 → survivable. > 3 → fundable. > 5 → underinvesting or wrong calc.
- CAC Payback < 12 months.

**SaaS Metrics:**
- MRR/ARR (revenue, not pipeline). Net Revenue Retention > 100% = best PMF signal.
- Gross Margin > 70%. Rule of 40 (growth% + margin% > 40). Magic Number > 1 (efficient growth).

**Business Model Canvas (Osterwalder):**
Partners / Activities / Value Prop / Relationships / Segments / Resources / Channels / Costs / Revenue.

### Product & Growth

**PMF Signals:**
- Weak: users don't return, NPS < 30, growth only via paid, churn > 5%/mo
- Strong: users complain when it's down, NPS > 50, organic > 40%, net retention > 100%
- Sean Ellis: > 40% "very disappointed" if product disappeared

**Growth Equation:**
Revenue = Traffic × Conversion × ARPU × Retention. Find the BOTTLENECK first. Improving conversion 2x on bad traffic = waste.

**Pricing (Value-Based):**
1. Customer's current cost of the problem? 2. Alternative solutions & prices? 3. Your unique value above alternatives? 4. Price = 10-30% of value created.
Cost-plus = lazy. Competitor-based = commoditizing. Can't articulate value in $ = can't price.

### Execution

**ICE Scoring:** Impact(1-10) × Confidence(1-10) × Ease(1-10). Do top 3. Kill the rest.

**2×2 Priority:**
```
           │ High Impact
  DO NEXT  │  DO NOW
  (plan)   │  (drop everything)
───────────┼─────────────
 ELIMINATE │  DELEGATE
  (say no) │  (automate)
           │ Low Impact
   Hard       Easy
```

**McKinsey 7S:** Strategy/Structure/Systems/Shared Values/Staff/Style/Skills must ALL align. Changing strategy without changing structure = failure.

## Case Studies (use as analogies)

### "We need to grow" → Decompose first
**Slack pre-IPO:** Framed as "growth slowing." Issue tree: acquisition fine (viral loop). Real bottleneck: enterprise conversion free→paid. Solution: Enterprise Grid + dedicated sales >500 seats. Result: enterprise revenue 40%→57%.

### "Should we enter market X?" → Reframe the market
**Figma vs Zeplin:** Zeplin's market = $200M (small). But design-dev collaboration = $2B+. Didn't compete on handoff — repositioned as "design platform." Dev Mode as upsell, not new product.

### "Pricing isn't working" → It's a segmentation problem
**Notion 2020:** Freemium too generous for individuals, too restrictive for teams. Fix: personal free forever, team $4→$8/user. Massive individual adoption → organic team conversion.

### "We're stuck in the middle" → Pick a lane
**WeWork vs Regus:** Premium experience + low price + massive scale = impossible unit economics. IWG survived by being boring and profitable. If you can't explain why you're NOT stuck in the middle in one sentence, you are.

### "We need a strategy" → Strategy = what you say NO to
**Stripe:** "Payments for developers" (narrow wedge). Owned ONE thing. Expanded to Billing/Atlas/Treasury only when wedge was dominant. Each expansion served existing customers. Your strategy doesn't kill 3 things you could do? It's a wish list.
